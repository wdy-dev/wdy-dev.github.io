My thoughts:

A different perspective on OpenAI Dev Day: The agency (GPTs) store is coming out to replace the plugin store. Why? Let me make some wild guesses.

  1. Plugins were rolled out quickly mainly to collect real-world use cases and data. GPT-4 showed its potential to be a general agent, but it’s only true when it can use all the tools (plugins) in the world.
  2. Even after fine-tuning on real data and extremely good engineering improvements, GPT-4 Turbo is still not capable of doing everything at once in real-world scenarios.
  3. Function calling improvements are and will be the most important updates for further releases. “GPT-4 Turbo is more likely to return the right function parameters,” but it’s likely not even close when you have 2000+ real-world tools.

Custom GPTs vs Assistant API

This is a more developer/startup question. Assistant API helps build agency into your app. Interface, data and profit is yours. Custom GPT is a agent itself. You get to share a little profit from OpenAI. But there is no bar. Everyone can pretty much do this. If you are thinking about an app idea from scratch using Assistant API, it is likely replaceable by a custom GPT. Same for app developer. Why would users pick your interface over ChatGPT? How can you keep your users in your app? OpenAI is obviously turning into a platform company, an operating system in the AGI world. This is not just an app on your phone.
